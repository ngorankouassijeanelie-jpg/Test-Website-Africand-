The Role

As an Analyst in Financial Structuring, you will work directly with our senior advisors on live mandates across West African agro-industrial transactions. You will be responsible for building and maintaining financial models, preparing DFI-standard investment files, and supporting the full transaction lifecycle from feasibility through to financial close.

Responsibilities

  • Build integrated project finance models (3-statement, debt sizing, DSCR, IRR) from first principles
  • Prepare information memoranda, financial summaries, and lender presentations
  • Conduct sector research and produce quantitative analyses on agro-industrial value chains
  • Support due diligence coordination with clients, technical experts, and legal counsel
  • Draft investment committee materials and respond to lender Q&A
  • Maintain transaction trackers and monitor milestone achievement across active mandates

Profile

  • Degree in Finance, Economics, Engineering, or a related quantitative discipline
  • Strong Excel/financial modelling skills, you can build a project finance model without a template
  • Genuine interest in West African agriculture, infrastructure, or development finance
  • Fluent in French and English (working language of transactions is bilingual)
  • Rigorous, detail-oriented, and comfortable managing multiple deadlines
  • Prior internship or experience in investment banking, DFI, or consulting is an asset

What We Offer

  • Direct exposure to complex, real transactions from day one, no siloed work
  • Mentorship from advisors with DFI and investment banking backgrounds
  • Front-row access to the West African agro-industrial financing ecosystem
  • Competitive compensation benchmarked against regional advisory standards
  • A small, high-performance team where your work has visible impact
